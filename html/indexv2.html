January 25, 2012
 

Faith. Academics. Service

It's time for one of our favorite annual traditions-Catholic Schools Week. From January 29 through February 5, the Catholic Schools Department (CSD) invites community members to join us for a variety of events, including open houses, masses, and fun activities. This is a great opportunity to mingle with parents and parishioners, meet our dedicated teachers and staff, check out our impressive schools, and see what our students have been up to so far this academic year.

This year's Catholic Schools Week theme is: Faith. Academics. Service. Together, these values are the pillars of our educational programs. One of the most rewarding aspects of my job is seeing these principles put into practice within our own school community. To celebrate this, we've added a couple of videos on our web site that show how The Catholic Advantage plays out in our schools. Also, if you missed it in our last newsletter, be sure to watch our students' "Dynamite!" video

Catholic Schools Week offers the perfect opportunity to step back and appreciate and recognize the edge our schools provide students, well beyond graduation. Take a look at these remarkable statistics:

  • 98% of Catholic high school students go on to college.
  • Graduates from Catholic high schools are likely to earn higher wages than public school graduates.
  • Catholic schools tend to produce graduates who are more civically engaged, more tolerant of diverse views, and more committed to service as adults.

These impressive numbers are something we should all be proud of.

I hope you will join us in celebrating Catholic Schools Week. To find out about events at your school, contact your school directly or visit csdsac.org

Sincerely,
Rick Maya
Director of Catholic Schools
