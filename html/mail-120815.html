August 15, 2012
 

Dear Catholic School Parents,

I am excited to welcome you back for the 2012-2013 school year! The Catholic School Department (CSD), principals, and teachers has been working diligently to implement many facets of our Strategic Plan that I first introduced to you in our May newsletter (you may view the Catholic Schools Strategic Plan here ). About one year ago, we restructured the Catholic School Department to efficiently align our resources in order to better support teaching and learning. These resources will ensure that our students will continue to experience a high quality Catholic education that prepares them for success in college and beyond. As always, this education is provided within an environment where our students learn to live and demonstrate the Gospel values of Christ.

College Success

As mentioned in the CSD Strategic Plan, we are adopting the California Common Core Standards (CCCS) in English Language Arts and Math. The CCCS are informed by the highest, most effective models used in America and around the world. These new standards will provide teachers and parents with a common understanding of what students are expected to learn. Consistent Standards will provide appropriate benchmarks for all students, regardless of where they live. More information regarding all of the content Standards can be found on our website. We will also transition immediately into a new means of assessing our students’ progress. We will no longer be administering the norm-referenced Iowa Test of Basic Skills (ITBS). This year we will be assessing student progress using a standards-based assessment program, Acuity, by CTB McGraw-Hill. Acuity will provide daily resources and information for teachers regarding your student’s comprehension of Standards taught. Additionally, all students in grades three through eight will be formally assessed four times a year. This data will give our Catholic School Department, principals, teachers, and parents the ability to monitor and measure students’ progress throughout the year so your child remains on target. You may learn more about Acuity here.

Parent Satisfaction—Strive for Excellence

In October, 2011, we introduced a monthly parent satisfaction survey. Our goal was to hear directly from you on how each individual school performs in various areas including Catholic identity, communications, academic course offerings, and campus safety. We also received your feedback on why you have chosen Catholic education for your child. What resonated highly among all parents was both Catholic identity and superior academics. I encourage you to view the diocesan summary of the annual survey results here. You may also ask your principal for your individual school’s results.

The Catholic Advantage Celebration

On September 8th, we will celebrate the Catholic Advantage. I invite all of our parents to join Bishop Soto and the Diocese of Sacramento for an evening of fun and reflection as we celebrate the success of our Catholic schools. We have an action packed evening planned that will include music, entertainment, dinner and a grand celebration commemorating our inaugural Catholic School Lifetime Achievement award. This year we will honor Monsignor James Kavanagh for his lifetime of commitment to Catholic education in our diocese. We will also honor the Christian Brothers order and thank them for their continued contribution to Catholic education in our diocese for the past 100 years. Space is extremely limited so I would encourage you to purchase tickets immediately and join us for a fun filled event. For more information, please visit tcfsac.org.

The Catholic Herald

Finally, beginning in January, I will be sending all of you a copy of the new Catholic Herald magazine. Seven times a year, The Catholic Herald magazine highlights important topics related to Catholic education, faith formation and other important topics relevant to the Catholic Church. Read month’s edition here.

The Time is Now

This is an exciting time for Catholic education here in the Diocese of Sacramento. We have a proud legacy of excellence in education as well as the faith formation of our youth. The key to sustainability will rely on our combined abilities as parents and educators to collectively create an environment that ensures Christian values, academic excellence and a spirit to serve our communities. The future of our Church and our Country relies on providing our students with an excellent education and teaching our youth to embrace the core values of Christ so that they are prepared to serve our communities with impact and compassion—that is the Catholic Advantage.

In Christ,

Rick Maya
Director of Catholic Schools & The Catholic Foundation
Diocese of Sacramento
