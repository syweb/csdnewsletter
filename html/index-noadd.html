January 14, 2012
 

Success starts here.

Welcome to the first edition of the Catholic Schools Department (CSD) newsletter, an opportunity to share, as an extended Catholic Schools family, some exciting things that are happening in our 42 diocesan schools and in the CSD. In it, you will find great student accomplishments, both inside and outside the classroom. We encourage you to share success stories for future newsletters!

2012 promises to be an exciting New Year for Catholic education. The CSD has been working hard to ensure that our schools are positioned for a great second half of the school year, as well as implementing a plan for long-term sustainability. The plan is simple: to become the best academic institutions in every neighborhood. Academic superiority combined with the Catholic identity of our schools and a commitment to giving back to the community is what will differentiate us from every other school across our vast region.

Over the past six months, many changes have taken place to guarantee that we have the necessary professional resources to ensure a stronger curriculum, better use of technology and improved classroom and parent communications.

In my new role as the Director of Catholic Schools, I am fortunate to see first-hand the high level of talent and dedication of our principals, teachers and staff. We are blessed to have them help shape the lives of our students. Our collective job is to ensure that our students are prepared to compete academically while living the gospel values of Christ in everything we do. We will be relentless in ensuring that our students receive a high quality Catholic education that prepares them for college and beyond. Simply put: we want to give them The Catholic School Advantage!

To kick-off our first edition, we've prepared a video that celebrates just how amazing our students are: they are simply "Dynamite!" Additionally, a sneak peek of some of the new marketing materials for the Catholic Schools of the Diocese of Sacramento can be viewed here.

May Jesus continue to bless our children, teachers and schools.

Sincerely,
Rick Maya
Director of Catholic Schools

School updates

» Academics • St. Vincent Ferrer School, Vallejo

Coached by Advanced Math teacher Ethel Tanchanco, the Junior High Math Team took First place in the Justin-Siena High School Math Competition against students from the Diocese of Santa Rosa.

» Achievement • St. James School, Davis

Sixth grader, Tyler Barry, received the American Red Cross Hometown Hero Award for saving the life of his young cousin.

» Charity • St. Francis High School, Sacra- mento

In a food drive for the Elk Grove Food Bank students collected 100,300 items. In addition to canned goods, students raised $6,600 for the food bank!

» Charity • St. John Vianney School, Rancho Cordova

Students raise over $4,000 for the American Heart Association by participating in Jump Rope for Heart.

» Service • St. John Notre Dame School, Folsom

Students and families are preparing and delivering freshly prepared food twice each month to the homeless at Friendship Park, reinforcing making service a part of their lives.

» Service • St. Vincent Ferrer School, Vallejo

Students in Mrs. Maria Landazari's first grade class wrote 375 Christmas cards to deployed members of the military, sharing their messages of thanks and Christmas cheer with those serving our country.

  

Annual Catholic Appeal

» Show your children the value of giving back. Support the Annual Catholic Appeal

This year more than 35,000 people will receive help and hope through programs supported by The Annual Catholic Appeal. Your gift assists 100 social service programs, seminarians training for the priesthood and Catholic educa- tion. In addition, 25% goes back to your parish for its own ministries to people in need. Bishop Soto tells us "God never tires of his generosity to us. Let us continue to celebrate God's faithfulness by being faithful to those who need our help." All Catholics in the Diocese of Sacramento are asked to give what they can. Ask your parish for a pledge envelope, donate online at www.diocese-sacramento.org or call 916.733.0266. Your gift truly makes a difference in someone's life.
